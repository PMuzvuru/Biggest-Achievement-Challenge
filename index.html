My Biggest Achievement

Graduating High School

"It always seems impossible until it's done." - Nelson Mandela

My Journey

As a matriculant at Hout Bay High School, I achieved four A’s, one B, and two C’s for my final matric results, despite facing difficult personal circumstances. My hard work and determination paid off as I scored high marks in my finals. I was honored at a provincial education department awards ceremony for excelling under these challenging conditions. On Tuesday, February 28 2023, I received an award from the Western Cape Education Department’s Metro Central Education District for my achievements. Throughout my high school years, I actively participated in many school programs and clubs, which further enriched my educational journey.

"Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world." - Nelson Mandela

Overcoming Challenges

As the mother of an 18-month-old son (at the time), my matric year was far from easy. The challenge of balancing school and motherhood left me constantly busy and with little time to study. My family circumstances added to the difficulty, with my father living in a different country and my mother working hard to support us. Living with extended family in a cramped, one-room space made finding a quiet place to study nearly impossible. Often, when I tried to focus on my books, my son would become restless. This meant I had to wait until late at night, when he was asleep, to get any studying done. Despite these tough times, I am deeply grateful for the support of my school principal, who provided a quiet and safe study space for matriculants.
